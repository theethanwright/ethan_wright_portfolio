PLAY
The "Play" exhibition, sponsored by für Gestaltung Zürich, is a traveling showcase designed for children, aiming to illuminate the universal nature of play rooted in modernist ideals. Accompanied by age-specific field guides, the exhibition encourages children to explore independently. The project seeks to demonstrate how Bauhaus and Modernist principles in toy design can transcend international boundaries, asserting that play is a universal language. The curated exhibit features works by toy designers from Czechia, America, Switzerland, Netherlands, and Germany, all active between 1920 and 1970. Embracing the Swiss international style, the team created guidebooks and an informative booklet for caregivers. The exhibition's identity, utilizing basic shapes and the Akzidenz Grotesk typeface, ensures a cohesive and recognizable experience across diverse cultures, aiming to provide insights into the interaction between design, children, and parental considerations.

This exhibit will be accompanied by guidebooks for children and an informative booklet for caregivers. In order to make an experience that is cohesive throughout cultures the exhibit and guidebook will be designed with the Swiss international style.
